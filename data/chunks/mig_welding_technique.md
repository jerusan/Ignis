# MIG Welding Setup and Technique

## Basic Wire Welding Technique

### Starting the Weld

1. Press (and hold) Trigger and contact the area to be welded with electrode wire to ignite arc.

### Weld Bead Types

2. For a narrow weld, you can usually draw the wire in a steady straight line. This is called a **_stringer bead_**.

   For a wider weld, draw the wire back and forth across the joint.

   This is called a **_weave bead_** and takes practice to perform properly.

### Wire Direction and Angles

3. Direct the welding wire straight into the joint. This gives an angle of 90° (straight up and down) for butt (end to end) welds, and an angle of 45° for fillet (T-shaped) welds.

4. For MIG welding using solid wire and shielding gas, the end of the MIG Gun should be tilted so that wire is angled anywhere in-between straight on and 15° away from the direction you are welding. The amount of tilt is called the **_push angle_**.

5. When using flux-cored wire without shielding gas, the end of the MIG Gun should be tilted so that wire is angled anywhere in-between straight on and 15° in the direction you are welding. The amount of tilt is called the **_drag angle_**.

### Contact Tip Distance

6. The Contact Tip should remain within 1/2″ of the work surface. This distance is called **_CTWD_** - Contact Tip to Work Distance.

## Overheating Protection and Cooling

> **Note:** If Welder is used too long, a warning screen appears in the LCD Display window and the unit automatically shuts down. The Welder automatically returns to service after cooling off. Should this occur, rest the MIG Gun on an electrically non-conductive, heat-proof surface, such as a concrete slab, away from the ground clamp.

## **Allow the Welder to cool with the power Switch on, so that the internal Fan will help cool the Welder.**

When normal operation resumes, use shorter welding periods and longer rest periods to help prevent needless wear.

## Welding Process and Quality Check

7. **_After welding the test weld on a piece of scrap for a few seconds,_** stop, and check your progress. Clean, then compare your weld's appearance with the diagrams and descriptions in the **Welding tips** section starting on the next page. After making any necessary adjustments, continue to weld **_while carefully following the DUTY CYCLE guidelines as explained on page 19._**

## **CAUTION! Weld will be hot, do not touch.**

## Shutdown Procedure

8. **When welding is complete, set the Mig gun down on a heat-proof, electrically non-conductive surface.** Turn the Power Switch OFF.

9. Allow Welder to cool down, then unplug the Power Cord.

10. Remove Ground Clamp from workpiece or table and disconnect MIG Gun.

## Post-Welding Wire Management

11. Respool wire by clipping wire, removing gas nozzle/contact tip on MIG gun, releasing Idler Arm on Wire Feed mechanism, and rotating the Wire Spool counterclockwise. Be sure to securely hold wire as it is being respooled because the end of wire has a tendency to quickly unravel once it clears the wire feeder.

## Gas System Shutdown (MIG Only)

12. **MIG Only:**

    **Close shielding gas cylinder valve securely.** Remove Regulator and replace cap. Disconnect Gas Hose from Welder. Store and secure gas cylinder.

## Duty Cycle Guidelines

**After practice welding for a few seconds, STOP and examine your weld using the guidelines starting on the next page.**
